          Prohibited items.

          Passengers are prohibited from carrying these items in either checked or hand baggage.

          The list of items are as follows:

          1.Explosives, munitions, fireworks, flares, Christmas crackers, sparklers, party poppers and pyrotechnics.

          2.Security-type cases/boxes incorporating goods such as lithium batteries or pyrotechnics.

          3.Compressed gases (flammable, non-flammable, or poisonous) such as butane, propane, aqualung cylinders, lighter fuels, or refills.

          4.Oxidizing substances such as bleaching powder and peroxides.

          5.Flammable liquids such as paints and adhesives

          6.Flammable solids such as safety matches and articles which are easily ignited

          7.Disabling devices such as mace or pepper sprays, with irritant properties.

          8.Poisons such as arsenic, cyanides, or insecticides

          9.Radioactive materials.

          10.Corrosive materials such as mercury (which may be contained in thermometers or blood pressure gauges,) acids, alkalis, and wet cell batteries.

          11.Meals-Ready-to-Eat (MREs).

          12.Firearms/parts of firearms such as revolvers, rifles, imitation firearms, pellet guns, ball-bearing guns, barrels, trigger assembly etc.

          13. Ammunition including blank, spent/empty cartridges

          14. Toy guns/gun shaped items or look-alike

          15. Any other substances which, during a flight, present a danger not covered above, such as magnetized, offensive, or irritating materials.

          Note: Failure to declare hazardous materials may result in civil or criminal penalties for passengers. All airlines are required to report violations to the relevant government authorities.

          * Carriage of electronic devices powered by lithium batteries in either checked or hand baggage are subjected to the specific conditions. For more information, please refer here .
